What It Is

MootMoat is an MIT-licensed open standard and architecture for agentic professional identity, designed specifically to help hardware and mechanical engineers survive automated AI screening. It compiles unstructured career exhaust into machine-readable endpoints while simultaneously producing human-readable outputs that survive legacy ATS gateways. You control the data. You host it. You own the entity.

---

The Evolution: From SaaS to Open Protocol

MootMoat's architecture was forged through a deliberate strategic pivot away from a centralized SaaS model. Forensic red-teaming revealed that uploading export-controlled or proprietary CAD logs to a cloud SaaS is a compliance nightmare. The current framework bypasses this entirely by recognizing the human brain as the primary archive: The Source.

You are not transcribing an old failure log. You are writing directly from the engineer who was in the room when it failed. No subpoena required. No air-gap breached. The scars are the receipt.

The solution was to abandon the SaaS model entirely and open-source the architecture. MootMoat is not a product you buy. It is a protocol you deploy.

The Hierarchy of Value

Level 1 conformance prevents Entity Resolution Failure. This is table stakes — the floor below which you are invisible to AI screening agents. It is worth having. Most engineers don't have it.

Level 3 is where differentiation begins. The Depth Signal makes genuine expertise legible to machines and memorable to humans. It does not manufacture an edge where none exists. If the expertise is there, the framework surfaces it. If it is not, no schema will create it.

The Two Core Mechanisms

**Coherence as the Signal** — solved by the Tri-Node Footprint. Maintaining synchronized, hyper-specific truth across your canonical domain, GitHub, and LinkedIn using the exact same jagged Depth Signals is the unfakeable signal. Bots do not maintain multi-substrate coherence of thermal resistance budgets and DFM tradeoffs. Humans with real constraints do.

**Translation of Lived Experience** — solved by Depth Signals. Depth Signals are the overarching genus of twelve techniques used to translate The Source into machine-readable and human-readable formats. The Structural Rhyme is the flagship technique — it feeds directly into the `isomorphicProof` block in the JSON-LD schema — but it is one of twelve. The full taxonomy with DIY prompts for each technique lives in `MootMoat_v17_Spec.md`. The Workbook (`MootMoat_v17_Workbook.md`) contains a canvas for each of the twelve.

The v17 Frontier: Compounding the Signal

While v16 solidified the individual extraction pipeline, v17 defines the architectural and empirical gaps that constitute the active roadmap:

**Reverse Radar (The Empirical Gap):** We do not guess how AI agents weight the schema; we measure it. Deploying structured data across sovereign domains allows us to capture behavioral telemetry on how parsing agents evaluate structured constraint data versus keyword density in the wild.

**Authenticated Endpoints (The Compliance Gap):** A future vector must define a secure routing mechanism for ITAR-adjacent constraint data to reach cleared reviewers without breaking the public schema or the air-gap requirement. Zero-knowledge proofs are one candidate approach. This gap is identified and named; it is not yet solved.

**Collaborative Graph Richness (Future Research Direction):** The Tri-Node Footprint proves individual coherence. A named hypothesis on the roadmap — not a current protocol capability — holds that two engineers independently referencing the same constraint project with consistent metadata could create a multi-entity coherence signal of a different order. This is under evaluation.

**Governance (The Taxonomy Gap):** Scaling the constraint domain taxonomy to photonics, MEMS, high-voltage, and acoustics requires a formal RFC process rooted in the Provenance Principle: the LLM formalizes, it does not discover.
