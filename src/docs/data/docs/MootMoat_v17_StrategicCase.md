Who This Document Is For

You are a hardware, mechanical, or systems engineer with ten, twenty, or thirty-plus years of shipped product behind you. Your career record includes thermal management crises resolved at 3am, yield failures diagnosed from failure mode evidence, ITAR-restricted programs you legally cannot describe in detail, and judgment calls that saved programs — or didn't. You know what you've done.

The 2026 hiring market increasingly does not. Not because your credentials are weak. Because your credentials are machine-unreadable by the AI agents that now gatekeep access to the roles worth having.

Section 1 — The Honest Assessment: Table Stakes vs. Differentiation

Before a human recruiter, hiring manager, or anyone with actual judgment sees your profile, an AI agent has almost certainly already evaluated it. This pre-screening phase — MootMoat calls it Round Zero — is where your application is either mapped onto a structured knowledge graph or silently discarded. You do not fail a screen. You are never screened at all.

Level 1 conformance prevents Entity Resolution Failure. It is table stakes — the floor below which you are invisible. It is worth having. Most engineers don't have it.

Level 3 is where differentiation begins. The Depth Signal makes genuine expertise legible to machines and memorable to humans. It does not manufacture an edge where none exists. If the expertise is there — if thirty years of constraint navigation has produced real cross-domain architectural judgment — the framework surfaces it. If it is not, no schema will create it.

The Depth Signal taxonomy contains twelve techniques. Each one is a different bet on the same proposition: that specificity at depth communicates cognitive architecture that generic content cannot replicate. A Provenance Chain, a Forensic Number, a Constraint Personality, a Decision Heuristic — these are not formatting choices. They are extractions. The Source is the raw material. The technique is the tool. The schema is the delivery mechanism.

Section 2 — The Agent Weighting Black Box (Reverse Radar)

What weight do AI screening agents actually assign to structured constraint domain data versus employment tenure? This is an empirical question, not a design parameter.

MootMoat does not claim to know the exact algorithm. The v17 architecture relies on Reverse Radar telemetry: deploying structured data across sovereign domains and monitoring how different agents parse and surface it. We are actively measuring the algorithm's behavior in the wild. We are generating actual market intelligence, not making assumptions about a black box.

This posture matters strategically. The engineers who deploy early are the ones generating the telemetry that will eventually characterize the black box. Early deployment is not just a personal career decision — it is participation in the empirical research that makes the protocol stronger for everyone who deploys it.

Section 3 — The Sovereignty Equation

The compounding dynamic is real. In a market where the protocol is new and adoption is low, the engineers who deploy early control a position that becomes harder to replicate as the standard normalizes.

The spec is MIT-licensed. The schema is open. The advantage is in two places, and no one can replicate either:

First, The Source. Your thirty years of constraint navigation, failure events, and decisions made under real consequences are yours alone. The schema gives your Source a delivery mechanism. It does not generate your Source.

Second, the execution. Deploying a Level 4 conformant profile — Tri-Node Footprint closed, all constraint domains with isomorphicProof blocks, careerVector populated, Reverse Radar active — is work. It requires extracting the right material, applying the right techniques, and maintaining coherence across independent substrates. That execution is not replicable from a template.

The laptop goes back. The Source does not. The scars are the receipt.

Section 4 — The v17 Frontier: What We Are Measuring and Building

v17 names four architectural gaps as an active measurement agenda:

**Reverse Radar** is the empirical layer. The protocol measures how agents evaluate structured constraint data versus keyword density in the wild. This is ongoing and generates real intelligence.

**Authenticated Endpoints** is a compliance gap. ITAR-adjacent constraint data cannot currently be routed securely to cleared reviewers through the public schema. A future vector must solve this — likely through encrypted JSON-LD payloads or zero-knowledge proofs — without breaking the air-gap requirement.

**Collaborative Graph Richness** is a named research direction, not a current capability. The hypothesis: if two engineers independently reference the same constraint project with consistent metadata, it creates a multi-entity coherence signal of a different order than single-node coherence. This is on the roadmap. It is not in the current spec, and no current deployment depends on it.

**Taxonomy Governance** is a structural gap. Scaling the constraint domain vocabulary to photonics, MEMS, acoustics, and high-voltage requires a formal RFC process. The governance model must defend the Provenance Principle: the LLM formalizes, it does not discover.
